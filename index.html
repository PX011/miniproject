Inbound Sales Data: Key Findings & Trends

An analysis of team performance over a 5-week period.

1. Team 1 Punches Above Its Weight

Team 1 is the model of efficiency. They receive only 29% of the total leads but are responsible for nearly 35% of all paid bookings. This proves they are exceptionally skilled at converting their opportunities into revenue.

2. High Lead Volume Masks Inefficiency

Team 4 receives the largest share of leads (33%) but only converts that into an equal share of bookings (34.7%). While not underperforming, they are not maximizing their advantage. Teams 2 and 3 are converting a smaller share of bookings than their lead share, indicating a clear efficiency gap.

3. Underperformance is a Systemic Issue

Teams 2 and 3 are significantly underperforming, generating less than half the revenue of the top teams. This wide performance gap suggests a systemic issue in training, process, or lead distribution that needs to be addressed urgently.

Metric Prioritization & Recommendations

Focusing on what matters.

Metric Prioritization

  1. Share of Bookings vs. Share of Leads (The True Efficiency Metric): This is the most important metric. It moves beyond simple conversion rates to show which teams are most effective at converting the opportunities they are given. Our goal should be for every team to have a positive gap, like Team 1.
  2. Revenue per Booking (The Profitability Metric): This is the second most critical metric. Once we improve efficiency, this metric ensures we are maximizing the value of every win. It is the key to profitability.
  3. Conversion Rate (The Foundational Metric): While secondary to efficiency and profitability, this metric provides a baseline for the overall health of the sales funnel. It helps diagnose broader issues but should be viewed in the context of the two primary metrics above.

Questions & Recommendations

  • Clone Team 1's Process Immediately: The top priority is to understand and replicate Team 1's "winning formula." We need to create a playbook based on their process and implement it as the standard for all teams.
  • Diagnose the High-Volume Problem: We must investigate why high lead volume leads to lower efficiency. Is it a lead quality issue from specific marketing channels, or is it a process breakdown under pressure? This requires an urgent deep dive with Team 4.
  • Launch a Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) for Underperforming Teams: Teams 2 and 3 require immediate intervention. They should be the first to adopt the new "Team 1 Playbook" and receive intensive coaching to bring their performance up to the new standard.
